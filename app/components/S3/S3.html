Why you may need better sleeping habits

  • Sleeping disorders are one of the most common forms of physical disorders.
  • About one in four persons is affected in Europe. About three out of five Americans report sleeping badly one or more times during the week.
  • Other estimates suggest at least one in ten people in Europe will be taking medication on any single night to help them sleep.
  • Categories of sleeping disorder include difficulties in falling asleep, difficulties in staying asleep during the night with constant interruptions / awakenings in the sleep wake cycle during the night and, not being able to fall asleep again once awoken in the very early morning hours.
  • There are enormous individual differences in terms of what ‘sufficient sleep’ actually means for people. Some people require three to four hours per night, whilst others require more than eight hours per night.
  • How much sleep do you really need to be really well? One simple way is for you to ask yourself: “How satisfied am I with
    1. the amount of sleep I am getting





    2. the quality of sleep that I am getting”?





  • A more systematic way is to create a ‘sleep log’ to gain a deeper understanding of how much sleep is optimal for you.
  • How to keep a sleep log
    1. Generally it is done over seven continuous days
    2. At the end of each 24 hour cycle you note in the log approximately how many hours you spent sleeping.
    3. You also note down if you felt the amount of sleep you had was right, too much or too little.
