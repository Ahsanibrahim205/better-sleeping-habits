How you can have better sleep

  • Here are two practices that are useful if you are the type of person who, when falling asleep, experiences physiological arousal changes / physical discomfort such as an acceleration of heart rate, increased temperature, bodily restlessness, increased movement or irritability.
  • Regular relaxation exercises before going to bed, playing a relaxation CD/ DVD when you are getting ready for bed or about to try to go to sleep.
  • You can also try these approaches to better sleep
    1. Scheduling physical activity for the late afternoon / early evenings.
    2. Making a habit of always getting up at the same time, even on days off.
    3. There is some evidence that eating sufficient poultry and drinking milk, both high in amino acids and tryptophan, may help you sleep better.
  • Creating the best physical conditions for you to sleep in, is your bedroom
    1. Exposed to minimal noise and light?
    2. Well ventilated?
    3. Is the temperature of the room too warm?
    4. Is the mattress sufficiently firm to avoid strain whilst lying down?
    5. Sex is, of course, a very natural activity to help you sleep well afterwards.
  • Things which can prevent you sleeping well
    1. Shortly before going to bed avoid eating heavy meals, light snacks may help in creating a relaxed state.
    2. Concentrated reading (because of the impact of physiological arousal) starts to make you think more, the reverse of what you are aiming to do
    3. Other similar forms of arousal whilst in bed, such as watching TV.
    4. Whenever possible, coffee or alcohol taken two to three hours before going to bed.
    5. If you take midday naps then you might find that in doing so you are not sleeping as well as you might at night time. >
  • If after about 30 to 40 minutes you still have difficulties falling asleep, then
    1. do not try to force yourself to go to sleep
    2. leave the bed, stand up and go and relax in another room or area
    3. do something quiet but relaxing such as listening to a CD / DVD.
  • You may try, before going to bed, these types of things
    1. taking / or giving yourself an occasional massage,
    2. taking a warm bath or shower shortly before going to bed
    3. enhancing this experience by using your favourite fragrance as soap or foam.
  • To help you feel really awake and raring to go in the mornings, you can take a shower during which you vary the water temperature.
